Cycling (in the broad sense of the word) is moving on the ground using vehicles (bicycles) driven by human muscular strength. Cycling includes such disciplines as racing on the track, highway, cross—country, mountain biking, competitions in figure riding and ball games on bicycles - velopolo and velobol, etc. Cycling is also part of a sport like triathlon. The main goal of racing disciplines is the fastest overcoming of a certain distance (and landscape) on a bicycle. The International Cycling Union (UCI), located in Switzerland, is the international regulator and organizer of international cycling competitions. At the national level, such functions are performed by relevant national organizations (in Russia — the Cycling Federation of Russia, in Ukraine — the Cycling Federation of Ukraine, etc.).According to the American Consumer Product Safety Commission, cycling is one of the most traumatic amateur sports. Cyclists are prone to injuries from abrasions and wounds sustained in a fall to severe traumatic brain injuries and multiple fractures. Cyclists are also characterized by injuries resulting from overloads and the position of the athlete while riding. Among them are tendinitis, muscle and joint pain, compression neuropathy of the median and ulnar nerves in the hands and genital nerves in the groin, pain in the cervical spine, arising from the need to constantly stretch the neck to look forward.
