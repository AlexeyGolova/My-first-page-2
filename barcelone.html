Barcelona is a city in Spain, the capital of the autonomous region of Catalonia and the province of Barcelona. The port on the Mediterranean Sea is 120 km from the French border and 504 km east of Madrid. The largest industrial and commercial center of Spain. One of the most important tourist destinations on European routes. The population of Barcelona is 1,608,680 people (INE 2016). It is the second most populous city in Spain after Madrid and the tenth in the European Union. In 1992, Barcelona hosted the Summer Olympic Games. In 2004, the World Cultural Forum 2004 was held in Barcelona. In March 2010, Barcelona became the capital of the Mediterranean Union, which includes 43 countries. In 2013, Barcelona hosted the World Aquatics Championships. There are two different legends regarding the foundation of Barcelona. The first attributes the foundation of the city to the hero of Greek myths Hercules 400 years before the foundation of Rome, and then in the III century BC. The city was rebuilt by the Carthaginian Hamilcar Barca, the father of Hannibal, who named the city Barsino in honor of his family. The second legend attributes the founding of the city directly to Hamilcar Barca. He founded a settlement at the foot of Manzhuik Hill. They proposed to name this camp in his honor (Barsino). In 133 BC, the Iberian city of Laie was conquered by the Romans led by Publius Cornelius Scipio Aemilianus. Around 15 BC, the Romans made a fortification out of the city, the center of which was located on Mount Taber, a small hill near the modern city hall (St. James Square). Under the Romans, the city was called Colonia Faventia Julia Augusta Pia Barcino, its value was small compared to the neighboring Tarracon (modern Tarragona). However, its wealth and importance grew over time due to its convenient location and beautiful harbor. The city minted its own coins, some of which have been preserved since the time of Galba. The remains of ancient fortifications can still be seen in the city today, and the typical Roman layout is still visible on the map of the historical center of the city, the so-called Gothic Quarter. So, Roman columns are visible on Paradis Street near the tour center of Catalonia. The city grew within the fortress wall until the conquest by the Visigoths in the 5th century. By this time, Barsino pushes Tarracon into the background.
